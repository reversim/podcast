<span style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.498039); color: #333333; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 20px;">Evolutionary algorithms open windows to the place where machines and biology meet.</span><br>
<span style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.498039); color: #333333; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 20px;">In this talk weI'll explore how evolutionary algorithms mimic and borrow from the way Mother Nature solves problems - all the way from solving puzzles, to social sciences to designing new kind of satellite antennas.</span><br>
<span style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.498039); color: #333333; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 20px;">We'll explore how we can model algorithms after the behaviors of different species in nature such as fireflies, Cuckoos and bees to solve everyday life problems.</span><br>
<span style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.498039); color: #333333; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 20px;">One cool hack we'll show is how you can use genetic algorithms to actually reverse engineer code for applications like search engines, or even chess algorithms. Last, we'll see how genetic algorithms can help solve NP hard problems such as the travelling salesman problem in new and creative ways.</span><br>
