<div style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.498039); box-sizing: border-box; color: #333333; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 20px; margin-bottom: 10px; word-break: break-word;">
Taking the role of a software architect for the last 10 years starting at a small startup moving to Amdocs OSS devision and then to Wix as chief architect, I have gained some understanding of what it makes to do architecture.</div>
<div style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.498039); box-sizing: border-box; color: #333333; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 20px; margin-bottom: 10px; word-break: break-word;">
I can say today that software architecture is not about</div>
<ul style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.498039); box-sizing: border-box; color: #333333; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 20px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-top: 0px;">
<li style="box-sizing: border-box;">UML</li>
<li style="box-sizing: border-box;">Those huge system box diagrams</li>
<li style="box-sizing: border-box;">Writing documents</li>
</ul>
<div style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.498039); box-sizing: border-box; color: #333333; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 20px; margin-bottom: 10px; word-break: break-word;">
I count 4 different types of software architecture - each of the four is complex and can take a full presentation by itself.</div>
<ul style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.498039); box-sizing: border-box; color: #333333; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 20px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-top: 0px;">
<li style="box-sizing: border-box;">System architecture - the actual layout of process on hosts - what is a service, number of instances, how services collaborate, etc.</li>
<li style="box-sizing: border-box;">Data architecture - the selection of data storage engines and their usage</li>
<li style="box-sizing: border-box;">Build architecture - the dependencies between different artifacts and their impact on development and deployment</li>
<li style="box-sizing: border-box;">Network architecture - the structure of your layer 1, 2 and 3 network with higher level services (Routers, VLANS, VPNs, etc).</li>
</ul>
<div style="background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.498039); box-sizing: border-box; color: #333333; font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 20px; margin-bottom: 10px; word-break: break-word;">
I propose talking about software architecture - what is it, what practices and challenges an architect should focus on and how to bring value to an R&amp;D organization. Resource management, self healing systems, containment of failure, caches, the symbiotic relation between architecture and organizational structure, etc.</div>
